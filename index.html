About the Candidate

Leadership that listens. Action that follows.

I am Ssendi Samuel MUBASA, running for Deputy Chairperson of the Makerere University Business School Guild. My candidacy is grounded in one conviction: students and staff deserve an association that works as hard as they do.

From Nakawa to Mbarara, from undergraduate to postgraduate, from faculty office to lecture hall — every member of our community deserves a seat at the table and a leader who turns conversations into outcomes.

  • Committed to inclusive representation across all faculties and campuses
  • Focused on measurable welfare improvements, not campaign slogans
  • Dedicated to transparent governance and accountable leadership

The Full Manifesto

Four pillars. One mission.

Every commitment below is designed to be tracked, reported, and delivered. No rhetoric — only results.

01

Unity

One voice. Every rank. Every faculty. Every campus.

  • Establish a cross-faculty student council that meets monthly to surface issues before they become crises.
  • Bridge Nakawa and Mbarara campuses with shared platforms, joint guild activities, and equal resource allocation.
  • Create faculty ambassadors so no programme — undergraduate, postgraduate, or diploma — is left unheard.
  • Promote dialogue between guild leadership, administration, and student representatives on a structured calendar.
02

Welfare

Real healthcare. Better working conditions. Science Pay.

  • Advocate for accessible on-campus and referral healthcare support for students and guild staff.
  • Push for improved working conditions for association employees — fair hours, timely remuneration, and safe environments.
  • Champion Science Pay and equitable compensation structures for research assistants and lab support staff.
  • Negotiate student-friendly welfare packages including hardship support and emergency response mechanisms.
03

Growth

Fair promotions. Research support. Professional development.

  • Work with faculty leadership to ensure transparent, merit-based promotion pathways for guild staff.
  • Establish a research support fund and mentorship programme connecting students with faculty and industry mentors.
  • Organise skills workshops, career fairs, and certification prep sessions every semester.
  • Partner with alumni and corporate sponsors to create internship pipelines for MUBS students.
04

Sustainability

An organised, accountable association that delivers.

  • Publish quarterly progress reports on every manifesto commitment — what was done, what is pending, and why.
  • Implement open-budget practices so members see how guild resources are allocated and spent.
  • Build institutional memory through documented handover protocols and digital archives.
  • Strengthen internal audit processes and member feedback loops to keep leadership accountable.
