Election Manifesto 2026–2028

Four pillars. Realistic commitments.

A message to every MUBASA member: I am building on the foundation those who served before me have laid — not starting from scratch. Four pillars. Realistic commitments. Honest reporting back to members.

01

Unity

One voice. Every rank. Every faculty. Every campus.

MUBASA is strongest when its members move as one body — every faculty, every rank, every campus: Main campus, Arua, Mbarara, Jinja, Mbale.

Collective Voice

  • Strengthen MUBASA's formal engagement with management so that staff positions are presented collectively, documented, and followed up on the record.
  • Represent members individually when navigating promotion processes, classification concerns, or workplace issues.
  • Maintain the collaborative relationship with management and continue engaging FASPU for national-level advocacy.

No Member Left Behind

  • Extend MUBASA communication and activities meaningfully to regional campuses, not just Nakawa.
  • Ensure every member knows their rights under the HR Manual, the Universities Act, and the Public Service Standing Orders.
  • Any member who faces workplace intimidation or harassment will have MUBASA formally and actively behind them.
02

Welfare

Real healthcare. Better working conditions. Science Pay.

Progress has been made on staff welfare. MUBASA's role now is to push for these gains to go further and faster, and to address the gaps that remain.

Salary Equity

  • Continue advocating through FASPU for the remaining salary enhancement for mid-level academic staff who have not yet received 100% of the approved harmonisation target.
  • Address the science vs arts salary classification anomaly at MUBS — a correction using the government's own structure, not a new salary demand.
  • Ensure RBS and NSSF contributions are remitted consistently and on time.

Healthcare

  • Push for the announced medical insurance policy to be operationalised quickly.
  • Advocate for coverage that includes staff and dependants — inpatient, outpatient, dental, and ante-natal care.
  • Support digitisation of the MUBS Health Centre and push for mental health support in the welfare package.

Working Environment

  • Work with management to formalise and improve staff transport for official activities.
  • Advocate for adequate office space and reliable internet infrastructure across all staff work areas.
03

Growth

Fair promotions. Research support. Professional development.

Staff growth is directly tied to the MUBS Strategic Plan 2025–2030. MUBASA's job is to make sure that commitment translates into real opportunity for every member.

Promotions That Move

  • Advocate for a published annual promotions calendar with clear timelines.
  • Push for a transparent process where eligible applicants are not held back informally.
  • Support access to a fair review process for staff with concerns about promotion evaluations.

Research and Academic Development

  • Advocate for equitable access to research funding across all faculties.
  • Push for structured mentorship linking junior and mid-career staff with senior researchers.
  • Ensure study leave processes are transparent and bonding terms do not discourage higher qualifications.

A Digital Home for MUBASA Members

  • Develop a member platform for association documents, welfare information, event updates, opportunities, and key policy summaries.
04

Sustainability

An organised, accountable association that delivers.

MUBASA will be here long after this term. Sustainability means the association future members inherit is stronger, better organised, and more trusted.

An Association That Works All Year

  • Hold scheduled general meetings consistently and publish minutes and follow-up actions within two weeks.
  • Maintain a member feedback channel so any staff member can raise an issue without attending a physical meeting.
  • Document ongoing engagements, commitments, and correspondence for incoming executives.

Financial Accountability

  • Ensure financial statements are presented clearly at annual general meetings.
  • Account transparently for every expenditure so members see their subscriptions are used in their interest.

Aligned with the MUBS Strategic Plan

  • Position MUBASA as a constructive partner in delivering the MUBS Strategic Plan 2025–2030.
  • Engage the next generation of MUBASA members so participation grows across all ranks and faculties.

I am not here to reinvent MUBASA. I am here to make it work better for every member. The policies are in place. The strategic plan is on the table. What is needed now is an association that follows through, keeps records, and reports back honestly. That is the job. I am ready for it.

— Ssendi Samuel MUBASA
